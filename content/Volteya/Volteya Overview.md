Volteya is a world built in fan fiction tropes. It features plot contrivances, omegaverse, and others all for the sake of its main theming

The Main Theme of Volteya is to show what things could be like if you made the smallest changes. The person  you could have become if you'd had different people in your life, or if the world had been different enough to make you someone else.

Volteya is a modern setting meant to be reminiscent of the early 2010s featuring all the trappings of the like such as phones and other modern technology. There's parodies present of real world things and the setting is meant to be vaguely North American so it feels almost like a splinter of the real world, mixed in with worldbuilding from Mytharia.
People can still be born lucent or aphotic here but don't possess visual differences as such. Vampires Werewolves, and Witches are all common as factions with tension between one another to play on the tropes of the supernatural present.

The setting itself is ruled by a god king known only as The Volt King. He is vaguely known to have reached apotheosis 2000 years ago and stunted the development of technology to keep it at this modern level. When he reached apotheosis in his first display of divine power he either accidentally or intentionally sundered what was one continent into two.

The setting features other lands beyond Volteya but their actual depiction isn't relevant to the story at hand other than as the setpiece that Volteya is a powerhungry nation ruled by a tyrant that wants to expand beyond his current domain, that he wants to expand to other parts of this planet through an endless war that keeps the citizens in fear and also placated by the notion of their military protecting them.

The Volt King has emissaries of his power, people he bestows with sparks of his power or artifacts he has created as his peacekeepers and his soldiers. The Bear King, Volt Action, and others each have something from him to flex their divine authority on behalf of god.

Volteya is a theocracy, they worship their god as their ruler and their ruler as their god.
