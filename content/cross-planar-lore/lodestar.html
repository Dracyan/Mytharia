Lodestars are a rare form of lucent beings whose sparks and souls possess the capability of crystalising physically into crystals and gems. These gems are then used to forge powerful items that only their lodestar can use to their fullest potential.

Description

Lodestars are the counterpart to Pharos'. A pharos externalises their soul and their spark through the manifestation of an incorporeal spirit which represents them. The abilities of said spirit might or might not be useful to the one who uses them. A Lodestar however, externalises their soul and their spark by wholly crystalising it outside their body into a gem. This gem which houses the essence of a lodestar's very being can be fashioned into a powerful item which allows the lodestar to transform and unleash their true power. But before one can do that, they must fashion their emblem.

The creation of an emblem can be an arduous process, but once complete the emblem is usually a small item worn by the lodestar, it could be earings, a necklace, a ring, or a myriad of other accessories. If a lodestar is departed from their emblem, the gem that makes up their soul, they will experience animaleipis.

An emblem, once created has a command word or phrase to activate or deactivate its power. While active an emblem grants its lodestar an attire fashioned from within, as well as a powerful weapon forged of the soul.

Once a lodestar is fully awakened and their emblem forged, in some cases spirits can be bound to the emblem, granting their power whenever their emblem is active and the spirit is temporarily absorbed inside.

Abilities

The abilities that are granted by a Lodestar's emblem are as varied as the abilities granted by a Pharos' shadow bitch but they all have two things in common.

When a lodestar's emblem is active, it grants them a unique attire, it could be armour, it could be a suit, it could be a robe, and it might change overtime with the person as they gain mastery over their emblem.

The second is a weapon. Sometimes this is as literal as a greatsword, or a bow, but other times it could also be a deck of cards or a yo-yo. This weapon is typically embedded with a large piece of the gem that makes up their emblem

Beyond this, the abilities granted by a lodestar's emblem vary as much as the lodestars who bare them. One might grant the ability to stop time, while another might grant the power of creation, or simply the ability to top-deck whatever card needed to win in a card game. It all depends on the souls and intentions of the lodestar and what guides them.

History

The first lodestar was recorded shortly after the shattering. all records have been lost as to who they were or what power they possessed beyond fragments of records that show an ornate crystaline yo-yo.

From there, lodestars have been a scarce but consistant occurance across mytharia. Often powerful leaders meant to protect the world, but in some cases also powerful villains bent on destruction.

It is believed a lodestars purpose is to lead with their soul, to let their soul be the star that guides those around them toward a better tomorrow. But what that better tomorrow looks like can be highly contested. Lodestars are often destined to congregate and fight for common causes, or to fight rivals with conflicting goals. They often guide the course of history with these conflicts and the resolutions that follow.
